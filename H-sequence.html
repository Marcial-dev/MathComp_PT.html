Mathematical Problem 1:
Formula: an=1/a1+(n-1)d
• Pedro has P10,000,000.00 in his bank account. In the first year he spent 1/20 of this, and in the next year he spent 1/19, and 1/18 in the third year. If he continues like this, how much would he spend in the 13th year? 		Given:
P10,000,000.00
a1=1/20
a2=1/19
a3=1/18
d= 19-20= -1

Solution: a13=1/20+(13-1)(-1)
a13=1/20+12(-1)
a13=1/20-12
a13=1/8
 • Pedro would spend 1/8 in the 13th year.
Mathematical Problem 2:
Formula: an=1/a1+(n-1)d
• Jesse decided to start doing pushups so he can achieve big muscles. On the first day he could only do 1/5 in a row, the second day he got stronger and started doing 1/12, on the third he was able to do 1/19 and so on. How much pushups has Jesse been able to do in a row on the 10th day? 		Given:
n = 10
a1 = 1/5
a2 = 1/12
a3 = 1/19
d: 7

Solution: a₁₀ = 1 / 5 + (10-1) 7
a₁₀ = 1 / 5 + (9) 7
a₁₀ = 1 / 5 + 63
a₁₀ = 1 / 68
 • Jesse was able to do 1/68 pushups in a row on the 10th day.
Mathematical Problem 3:
Formula: an=1/a1+(n-1)d
• A rubber ball is dropped from the second floor and bounces back, reaching various heights that slowly decrease over time. The first bounce reaches a height of ⅓, the on the second bounce, it reaches a height of ⅕ but suddenly pops. Now assuming this pattern continues and the ball did not pop, what would its height be when it bounces for the fourth time? 		Given:
A₁ = 1/3
A₂ = 1/5
d = 2
n = 4

Solution:
A₄ = 1/3 + (4-1)2
A₄ = 1/3 + (3)2
A₄ = 1/3 + 6
A₄ = 1/9
 • The rubber ball's height would be 1/9 when it bounces for the fourth time.
